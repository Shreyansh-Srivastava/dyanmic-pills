COURSE ACTIVITY NAME: ENGINEERING DESIGN AND INNOVATION

A group activity in which students has to design an innovative project

Introduction

About:

It is a group activity which students has to perform every semester in a group of 4 to 6 where the students gets an oppourtunity to showcase their skills and develop an industry ready innovative product (project). The project can ethier be hardware or software based on student interests and each group is assigned with a faculty as a guide who looks after their progress every week. It is a credit course which is included in the syllabus of students every semester.

Outcomes:

  1. Collaboration skills: Group projects can help students develop their ability to work with others, communicate effectively, and build relationships. These skills are essential in the engineering field, where teamwork is often required to complete complex projects.
  2. Division of labor: Group projects allow students to divide the work among themselves and specialize in areas of their interest and strengths. This division of labor can lead to a more efficient use of time and resources, as well as better overall results.
  3. Diverse perspectives: Working with other students who have different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives can bring new ideas and solutions to the project. This can help students develop their critical thinking skills and broaden their understanding of the subject matter.
  4. Real-world experience: Group projects can simulate the experience of working on a team in a professional setting. This can help prepare students for their future careers in engineering and give them an opportunity to apply the concepts they have learned in class.
  5. Improved problem-solving skills: Group projects often involve tackling complex problems that require innovative and creative solutions. This can help students develop their problem-solving skills and learn to think outside the box.

COURSE ACTIVITY NAME: SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT PROJECT

An individual activity in which student has to design an innovative software project

Introduction

About:

It is an individual activity which student has to perform every semester where student gets an oppourtunity to showcase his/her skills and develop an industry ready innovative product (project). The project should be on a software based domains as per student interests and each student is assigned with a faculty as a guide who looks after their progress every week. It is a credit course which is included in the syllabus of student every semester.

Outcomes:

  1. Real-world experience: Software development projects can simulate the experience of working on a software development team in a professional setting. This can help students develop their skills in software development, project management, and teamwork, giving them an opportunity to apply the concepts they have learned in class.
  2. Agile project management: Agile project management is widely used in software development, and learning to apply agile methodologies can prepare students for the fast-paced and iterative development processes used in the industry. Working on a software development project can help students develop skills in agile project management, including planning, backlog management, and sprint execution.
  3. Specialization: Software development projects allow students to specialize in areas that interest them, such as front-end development, back-end development, or database development. This division of labor can lead to a more efficient use of time and resources, as well as better overall results.
  4. Hands-on experience with development tools: Software development projects can give students hands-on experience with the tools and technologies used in the industry, such as integrated development environments (IDEs), version control systems, and testing frameworks.
  5. Professional development: Working on software development projects can help students develop their professional skills, such as time management, problem-solving, and communication. These skills can help them succeed in their future careers in software development.

COURSE ACTIVITY NAME: COURSE PROJECT

A group activity in which students has to design a project using concepts taught in course

Introduction

About:

It is a group activity in which students has to perform every semester for all the courses taught in the semester where each student gets an oppourtunity to practically implement the concepts that are taught in class. The project should be based domains which are included in the syllabus and lab faculty looks after the progress of projects within lab sessions. It has some weightage of marks and each student has to make this project for all courses in every semester.

Outcomes:

  1. Active learning: Project-based learning encourages students to actively engage with the material, allowing them to apply what they have learned to real-world problems. This can lead to a deeper understanding of the subject matter and better retention of the material.
  2. Authentic assessment: Project-based learning allows for authentic assessment, meaning that students are evaluated based on their ability to apply what they have learned to real-world problems. This can provide a more accurate assessment of their knowledge and skills than traditional exams and quizzes.
  3. Problem-solving skills: Project-based learning involves solving real-world problems, which can help students develop their critical thinking and problem-solving skills. This can prepare them for the challenges they may face in their future careers in engineering.
  4. Creativity and innovation: Project-based learning allows students to be creative and innovative in solving real-world problems. This can help them develop their ability to think outside the box and come up with unique solutions to complex problems.
  5. Collaboration skills: Project-based learning requires collaboration between students, allowing them to develop their communication and teamwork skills. These skills are essential in the engineering field, where teamwork is often required to complete complex projects.

COURSE ACTIVITY NAME: DESIGN THINKING

A group activity or individual activity in which students has to publish reasearch paper on their projects

Introduction

About:

It is a group activity or individual activity which students has to perform every semester in a group of 4 to 6 or individually where the students has to publish their research work or project work that they did in the complete semester. The students can ethier publish their research paper or file a patent for their projects. Each group is guided by the EDI guide of that group for the complete process of publication. It is a credit course which is included in the syllabus of students every semester.

Outcomes:

  1. Recognition: Publishing research papers can lead to recognition and visibility within the academic and professional communities. It can establish the author's credibility and reputation, which can help in career advancement and networking.
  2. Career advancement: Publishing research papers can help in career advancement, such as getting promoted or securing a new job. It can demonstrate the author's expertise, knowledge, and experience in the field of engineering.
  3. Access to funding: Publishing research papers can increase the author's chances of securing funding for future research projects. It can demonstrate the author's ability to conduct research and contribute to the field, which can help attract funding agencies and investors.
  4. Personal growth: Publishing research papers can promote personal growth and development. It can help authors develop their critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills, as well as enhance their understanding of the field of engineering.
  5. Contribution to the field: Research papers can contribute new knowledge, theories, and insights to the field of engineering. It can also help address existing gaps and challenges in the field, leading to further research and development.
